Chapora Fort

Chapora Fort gained its popularity after the movie
“Dil Chahta Hai”
Administrator
09.02.2023

The Chapora Fort, sometimes referred to as Dil Chahta Hai Fort, is located on the northern shore of the island of Goa. What Amir Khan, Akshaye Khanna, and Saif Ali Khan felt in 2001 is still present in you. This fort is only 700 metres from Vagator Beach and looks out over the Chapora river. Make an effort to schedule your stay at Aiden Vagator by Best Western so that you can visit both of these tourist destinations whenever you are there.

History

Overlooking the Chapora River near Bardez, Goa, stands the Chapora Fort. The Muslim king Adil Shah's palace was replaced by a fort called Shahpura, which the Portuguese called Chapora. With views of Pernem, Vagator, the Chapora River in the north, and the Arabian Sea in the west, it has become a popular tourist attraction.

The fort, which is currently in ruins, is one of the most well-liked tourist locations in Goa despite its interesting past, unquestionable majestic attractiveness, and gorgeous location close to the sea. It is a popular viewpoint for nature lovers, as well as one that lovers of history and architecture frequently visit. A large chunk of the fort's original construction still exists in the form of its ramparts and outer walls of the fort still make up a sizable portion of its original structure.
