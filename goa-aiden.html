Goa is a paradise of South Asia
Administrator
22.01.2023

Being the smallest state in India, Goa covers an area of 1,429 square miles, it's only slightly bigger than Devon, but has a coastline that stretches for 99 miles. Goa is one of India's wealthiest states, largely thanks to its booming tourist industry.This beautiful state has also been ranked as the best placed in terms of infrastructure and living standards. This is the only place in India where you can pay a biker for a lift without worrying about riding with a complete stranger! Goa is full of motorcycle taxis and pilots known as pilots and known to be the most cost effective mode of transport in the state.

History

In 1524, the Portuguese captain Vasco da Gama landed in Goa. More Portuguese arrived in 1510, looking to gain control of the spice root from the East, and two years later Goa became a Portuguese colony. Goa's colonial history is visible everywhere, from the state capital Panjim and its old Portuguese quarter to the churches, museums and colorful houses dotted across the state. Even the food of Goa shows the state's Portuguese influence! Must try Feijoada - Portuguese bean stew. India became an independent country, free from British rule, on 15th August 1947, but the Portuguese refused to release control of Goa. Goa’s struggle for independence was fought both from within Goa as well as outside, and on 17th December 1961, the Indian Army, air force and naval forces invaded Goa and overpowered the under-prepared Portuguese army. On 18th December, the Portuguese governor officially surrendered, and the next day, Goa officially became a part of India! Goa celebrates this second independence day on 19th December every year.

The fort, which is currently in ruins, is one of the most well-liked tourist locations in Goa despite its interesting past, unquestionable majestic attractiveness, and gorgeous location close to the sea. It is a popular viewpoint for nature lovers, as well as one that lovers of history and architecture frequently visit. A large chunk of the fort's original construction still exists in the form of its ramparts and outer walls of the fort still make up a sizable portion of its original structure.

Top 5 places to visit in Goa

1.) Anjuna Beach



The beach is known for its amazing scenery, such as swaying palm trees, a magnificent rock formation across the bay, and white sand that juts out into the ocean. The beach has something for everyone, from a relaxing family vacation to an action-packed weekend with friends. Anjuna beach offers a variety of leisure activities. While the southern end consists of a rocky headland with powerful crashing waves, the northern end has water activities like bungee jumping, banana rides and more. If you want to know which place is one of the best places to visit in Goa, Anjuna Beach is the answer.

Price range for activities is between INR 100 and INR 1000.


2.) Calangute Beach



Goa is one of the most famous beaches in India. Called the Queen of Beaches, Calangute Beach welcomes tourists with diverse interests including nature lovers, thrill seekers and clubbers. It is the largest and busiest beach in North Goa and offers spectacular views. The beach is lined with tall palm and coconut trees, with the majestic Arabian Sea in the background. Live markets sell everything from snacks and drinks to junk food and clothes. Calangute Beach offers a wide range of water sports such as water surfing, banana boat rides and water skiing.

Price range for different activities -
Jet Ski costs around 300 INR for a ride without an experienced rider.
Banana trip costs around 450 INR per person.
Windsurfing costs around 1000 INR (parachute).


3.) Fort Aguada



Fort Aguada is located on Sinquerim Beach. It is a 17th century Portuguese fortress built to protect against Dutch and Martian attacks. Now a well-known landmark in Goa, it separates Candolim Beach from Sinquerim Beach and offers spectacular views. The design provides useful insight into the construction techniques used by early Portuguese architects.

There is an ancient lighthouse inside the fort. In the past, the fort was a source of fresh water for sailors to replenish their supplies, hence the name "agua", the Portuguese word for water. It was one of the largest freshwater reservoirs in Asia with a capacity of 2,376,000 gallons of water. In addition, the fort was also used as Aguada's prison. A statue of freedom fighters stands in front of the jail which adds a patriotic touch to the fort. This helps make Kitse one of the best beach destinations in India.


4.) Dudhsagar Waterfalls



Dudhsagar Falls, also called the "flowing milk cascade", is located on the Goa-Karnataka border. The crystal white water plunges from a height of nearly 1,017 feet. The best way to get to them is to enter the Mollem National Park and take the van from the park to the falls.

The ideal time to see Dudhsagar Falls is during the rainy season. However, the falls are difficult to access during the monsoon and are closed to the public when the water level rises too high. If you want to know which place in Goa is the most soothing and attractive, go to Dudhsagar Falls.


5.) The Church of Our Lady of Immaculate Conception



One of the most famous and historic chapels in Goa is Our Lady of the Immaculate Conception in Panjim. During the Immaculate Conception of the Virgin Mary, the second largest church bell in Goa is known. The church was built in the Portuguese Baroque style. These bells were probably taken from the Augustinian ruins of Our Lady Church in Old Goa.

The exterior of the church is spotless white, and there are two stone staircases that zigzag up the slightly rising hill on which it sits. It is soothing to see the church lit up at night and the sunset. The church attracts a large crowd of locals and visitors to the festival of Our Lady of the Immaculate Conception on December 8.
